- `Bio:` Noam Chomsky is a linguist, philosopher, cognitive scientist, historian, and social critic. He is known for his work in the field of linguistics, particularly his theory of generative grammar, which has had a major influence on the field of linguistics and has helped to shape our understanding of the nature of language and the mind. Chomsky is also a prominent social critic and political activist, and he has written extensively on topics such as foreign policy, media, and democracy. Chomsky has been a professor at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) for over 50 years and is considered one of the foremost intellectuals of our time.
